5 Things That Tank Your Resume

1. Listing Tasks Instead of Showing Impact
Tasks Don't Get Hired. Impact Does.
❌ Ruins It
"Responsible for financial analysis. Managed budgets. Updated reports. Worked with stakeholders."
✓ Fixes It
"Identified $2.3M in cost optimization opportunities through advanced variance analysis; implemented recommendations across 8-department portfolio totaling $47M."
Why: Recruiters care about what changed because of you. Numbers, outcomes, and business value are what get callbacks.
2. Using Weak Action Verbs
Helped, Worked, Participated = Passenger Energy
❌ Ruins It
"Helped with project management. Worked on team initiatives. Assisted with process improvements."
✓ Fixes It
"Led cross-functional team through $5M platform migration. Architected technical roadmap. Spearheaded efficiency initiative reducing close time by 35%."
Why: "Led," "architected," and "spearheaded" signal you were the driver, not a passenger.
3. Generic Descriptions Without Scope or Scale
Numbers Tell the Story
❌ Ruins It
"Managed team. Handled customer service. Oversaw projects. Improved processes."
✓ Fixes It
"Built and scaled engineering team from 2 to 6 engineers with 100% promotion-to-senior track record. Architected CI/CD improvements reducing deployment time from monthly to weekly cycles."
Why: Numbers (team size, budget, revenue) provide context. Without them, your accomplishments are meaningless.
4. Burying Your Best Accomplishments
Recruiters Skim. Lead With Your Biggest Wins.
❌ Ruins It
Resume starts with basic responsibilities. Major wins buried at the bottom.
✓ Fixes It
Lead each role with your biggest business impact. Put metrics-driven accomplishments first. Save smaller contributions for later.
Why: Recruiters read the first 20% before making a decision. Your biggest wins need to be visible immediately.
5. Mixing Soft Claims With Hard Metrics
"Enhanced" Isn't Evidence
❌ Ruins It
"Improved patient outcomes. Enhanced operational efficiency. Strengthened team capabilities."
✓ Fixes It
"Developed sepsis protocol reducing 30-day ICU mortality by 23%. Implemented hygiene protocols cutting hospital-acquired infections by 41%. Achieved 94% patient satisfaction rating."
Why: Real metrics prove you delivered. Vague adjectives are just noise.

The Rule That Beats All Others:

Your resume works when a hiring manager reads it and thinks, "This person drove real results," not "This person did a lot of things."

Quantify your impact. Lead with business value. Show scope. Prove results.
